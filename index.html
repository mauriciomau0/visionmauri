About me

Where machine learning
meets the real world

Driven by a passion for applying artificial intelligence to human performance and public health, I pursued a BSc in Biomedical Engineering from the Universidade Federal do Pará and completed my PhD in AI for Human Performance at the Technological University of the Shannon.

My work sits at the intersection of machine learning and health — synthetic data generation, performance monitoring, and computer vision applied to movement analysis. I care about methods that hold up under real conditions, with real constraints. I've worked in resource-limited settings long enough to know that's where most of the interesting problems live.

I work in English and Portuguese, and can get by in Spanish.

My Work Style

I do my best work on problems that don't fit neatly into one discipline. Statistical modelling and AI are tools — the point is a decision someone can actually act on. That means constantly translating between technical and non-technical audiences, which I've come to think of as part of the job. In teams, I try to identify people's strengths and help them enhance their skills.

This portfolio covers my work in AI, data science, and human performance research — across sports science, clinical settings, and public health.
