Where machine learning meets the real world

Driven by a passion for applying artificial intelligence to human performance and public health, I pursued a BSc in Biomedical Engineering from the Universidade Federal do Pará and completed my PhD in AI for Human Performance at the Technological University of the Shannon.

My work sits at the intersection of machine learning and health — synthetic data generation, performance monitoring, and computer vision applied to movement analysis.

My Work Style

I do my best work on problems that don't fit neatly into one discipline. Statistical modelling and AI are tools — the point is a decision someone can actually act on.
